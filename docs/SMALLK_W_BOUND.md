## 0. Claim (assembled theorem, per branch)

Let T be a k-uniform edge-to-edge tiling by unit regular polygons of the family
{3,4,6,12} ∪ {4.8.8}, k ≤ 3, with period lattice Λ. Then, in the 12-direction step alphabet
(24-direction only for the 4.8.8 case):

| branch (shape of Λ) | k=1 | k=2 | k=3 | form the solver consumes |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| hexagonal | **6** | **8** | **10** | Gauss basis (u, ωu), both in W(R) — pairs suffice |
| square | **3** | **6** | **7** | Gauss basis (u, iu) — pairs suffice |
| rect/rhombic/oblique (hol ≤ 4) | 7 | 15 | 23 | generators in W(2n−1) + join closure (thm:weight verbatim, n ≤ 4k) |
| 4.8.8 (k=1 only, ζ₂₄) | 3 | — | — | u = 1+√2, basis (u, iu), wt = 3 (analytic) |

Measured truth (oracle, all 92 tilings k ≤ 3): 5, 6, 7 — and §5b proves these are EXACT
(attained, with lower-bound certificates). The rigid-branch slack (6 vs 5, 8 vs 6, 10 vs 7) is
census overcount, refinable (§6); every value above is already solver-feasible
(|W(10)| ≈ 1.9e4, |W(23)| ≈ 4.6e5 in 12 directions, before norm filters).

## 1. Inputs cited from the thesis (proven there, not re-proven here)

- **lem:wallpaper**: k-uniform tilings have a wallpaper symmetry group, in particular a rank-2
  period lattice Λ; and (δ₀ = 1) distinct vertices are at distance ≥ 1, hence λ₁ ≥ 1.
- **lem:quotient**: |V(Q)| ≤ k·|P|; Q = vertex classes mod Λ. Separately, |P| ≤ hol(Λ)
  (proven inside prop:orbitfloor: P is the group of linear parts, injective since Λ carries all
  translations, and preserves Λ since Λ ⊴ G, so P embeds in the holohedry as a subgroup —
  whence L1's stronger "|P| divides hol(Λ)" by Lagrange). hol(Λ) ∈ {2, 4, 8, 12} is the
  classical Bravais classification of plane lattices.
- **lem:voltage / thm:weight**: Λ is generated by fundamental-cycle voltages, each of weight
  ≤ 2|V(Q)|−1; each edge contributes one unit root of the tiling's edge-direction set.
- **Lattice systems**: oblique (hol 2); rectangular/rhombic (4); square (8); hexagonal (12).
  Degenerate rhombic lattices at 60°/90° have hol 12/8 and belong to the hex/square branches.
- **12-direction regime**: the octagon occurs only in 4.8.8, which is rigid and 1-uniform
  (settled decision, CLAUDE.md); so for k ≥ 2 — and for k = 1 outside 4.8.8 — all edge
  directions lie on one 30° grid and Λ ⊂ ℤ[ζ₁₂].
- **Corner-share identity**: |det Λ| = A = Σ over vertex classes of share(VC), where
  share(VC) = Σ corners (tile area)/(tile corner count). Exact share·12 values:
  triangle (0,1), square (3,0), hexagon (0,3), 12-gon (6,3) in (p, q) ≘ p + q√3 coordinates —
  all nonnegative.

## 2. New lemmas (proven here)

**L1 (orbit census).** Each of the ≤ k vertex orbits under G contributes m_i = |P|/|Stab_i|
translation classes (Stab_i ∩ Λ = 1 and Stab_i ↪ P, so m_i divides |P| by Lagrange), all of one
VC species. Hence A = Σ_{i≤k} m_i·share(VC_i) with |P| dividing hol(Λ), and on the quotient
torus V − E + F = 0 with E = ½Σ m_i·deg_i and F = Σ_p t_p, t_p = (Σ p-corner count)/p ∈ ℤ.
The species are the 10 multisets over {3,4,6,12} with interior angles summing to 2π
(angle units of 30°: 3↦2, 4↦3, 6↦4, 12↦5; enumerated mechanically).
The quotient is a genuine CW torus — a nonzero translation cannot fix a bounded tile (interior
overlap) nor reverse an edge (2v = 0) — so the handshake and Euler identities apply verbatim,
with loops and multi-edges handled by the counting form. (Referee note: given angle-validity
and t_p ∈ ℤ, Euler holds identically — the binding filters are the parity and per-p
integrality conditions; Euler is kept as belt-and-braces.)

**L2 (shell finiteness).** For x = a+bζ+cζ²+dζ³ ∈ ℤ[ζ₁₂] (ζ = ζ₁₂),
|x|² = X + Y√3 with X = a²+b²+c²+d²+ac+bd, Y = ab+bc+cd. The exact identity
2X − (a²+b²+c²+d²) = (a+c)² + (b+d)² ≥ 0 gives X ≥ ½(a²+b²+c²+d²), so each exact shell
{x : |x|² = X+Y√3} is finite with coefficients bounded by √(2X). Density of ℤ[ζ₁₂] in ℂ is
irrelevant once the norm is pinned. (Referee cross-check: the BFS weights agree with the
independently derived closed form wt(a₀+a₁ζ+a₂ζ²+a₃ζ³) = f(a₀,a₂) + f(a₁,a₃),
f(p,q) = min_s |p+s|+|q−s|+|s|, on all 4,140 shell elements.)

**L3 (pure-species rigidity, five species).** If every vertex of T has species s, and
s ∈ {3⁶, 4⁴, 6³, 3.12.12, 4.6.12}, then T is the corresponding uniform tiling (hence k = 1,
with n and shell as tabulated below). *Proofs.* 3⁶/4⁴/6³: single tile type; a regular tile
placed on a given full edge, on a given side, is unique, so the whole tiling is forced from any
one tile; the regular tiling results. 3.12.12: the vertex word admits adjacent pairs (3,12) and
(12,12) only, so no two triangles share an edge — every neighbor of a triangle is a 12-gon —
and at each vertex of a 12-gon its two edges neighbor one 3 and one 12, so edge-neighbors
around every 12-gon alternate 3, 12 (12 edges, even — consistent; the alternation phase of a
12-gon is fixed by any single one of its neighbors). 4.6.12: pairs (4,6), (6,12), (4,12) only,
no same-type pair; neighbors alternate 6,12 around each square, 4,12 around each hexagon,
4,6 around each 12-gon (all even edge counts; phase again fixed by one neighbor).
*Propagation (both cases):* T contains a 12-gon (every vertex has one); the uniform tiling U is
transitive on its 12-gons, so position U by an isometry matching one 12-gon of T.
Induct on distance r in the tile-adjacency graph from that tile: a tile at distance r+1 shares a
full edge with a matched tile t at distance r; its TYPE is forced (by t's alternation pattern,
whose phase was fixed when t was matched, or trivially when t is a triangle/all neighbors one
type), and a regular tile of known type on a known full edge and side is uniquely placed — so it
matches U. The tile-adjacency graph is connected, so T = U. U is vertex-transitive (its named
symmetry group acts transitively on vertices — the standard structure of these uniform tilings),
so k = 1. ∎
True census entries of the five: 3⁶ n=1 shell (1,0)_hex; 4⁴ n=1 (1,0)_sq; 6³ n=2 (3,0)_hex;
3.12.12 n=6 (7,4)_hex; 4.6.12 n=12 (12,6)_hex.

**L4 (co-occurrence connectivity).** Build C(S) on the tile types of the census species set S:
t ~ t′ iff some s ∈ S contains both. Two tiles sharing an edge are both incident to both of its
endpoints, so their types are co-present in the endpoint VCs and are C-adjacent. For
**edge-to-edge, locally finite** tilings by disks the full-edge tile-adjacency graph is
connected (perturb a path between two tile interiors off the vertex set; each transversal edge
crossing switches between the two tiles sharing that full edge — note this fails for
non-edge-to-edge tilings, e.g. running-bond brick, hence the hypotheses). Both hypotheses hold
here, so C(S) restricted to the types present must be connected; census combos with
disconnected C(S) are unrealizable. ∎ (This kills, e.g., all {3⁶, 6³} mixes.)

**L5 (rigid basis form).** Let Λ be hexagonal-shaped and u a minimal vector. hol(Λ) contains
the 60° rotation, so ωu ∈ Λ (ω = e^{iπ/3} = ζ₁₂²), and ⟨u, ωu⟩ ⊆ Λ. Equality: reduce any
x ∈ Λ modulo ⟨u, ωu⟩; the remainder lies within the covering radius |u|/√3 < |u| = λ₁ of the
triangular lattice ⟨u, ωu⟩, and is itself in Λ, hence 0. So (u, ωu) is a basis, and
wt(ωu) = wt(u) since multiplication by a unit root permutes the step alphabet. Square case
identically with i = ζ₁₂³ and covering radius |u|/√2 < |u| (this also covers the 4.8.8 row's
(u, iu) in ζ₂₄). Hence W(Λ) ≤ wt(u), and the norm is pinned: |u|² = (2/√3)A (hex), = A
(square). In census coordinates A = (P_A + Q_A√3)/12 gives (X, Y) = (Q_A/6, P_A/18) (hex)
resp. (P_A/12, Q_A/12) (square) — nonnegative since all share coordinates are, and by L2 a
realizable |u|² has INTEGER (X, Y), so census combos failing the divisibility are unrealizable
as hex/square lattices and are soundly dropped (they remain covered by the other branches'
pools, which are taken as a union). ∎

## 3. The computation (exact integers throughout)

`experiments/results/smallk-census-shells-2026-07-10.csv` + generating script (same directory).
Pipeline: enumerate census combos per L1 (|P| over divisors of 12 resp. 8; r ≤ k orbits;
m_i | |P|; Euler + tile-count integrality), drop pure-rigid multi-orbit combos (L3) and
C-disconnected combos (L4), add the five true rigid shells; map to shells per L5 (integrality
filters); enumerate every shell element (L2 coefficient bound, boundary asserted); compute
wt exactly by BFS over the 12 unit directions (all shells resolved by depth 10); report the
per-shell maximum (sound: the tiling's u is *some* element of its shell; all six minimal vectors
share one weight, but distinct rotation classes on one shell may differ — the max covers all).
