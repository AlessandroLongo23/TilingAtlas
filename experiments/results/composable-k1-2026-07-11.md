# Composable tiles — k=1 results (2026-07-11)

DEMO of Čtrnáct palette-agnosticism. **NOT a completeness result.** Two caveats govern every
number here:

1. **Decomposition ambiguity.** With regular and composite tiles in one palette, a single geometric
   tiling is representable many ways (a rhombus tiling == a triangle tiling with shared edges
   erased). So these counts are over each palette's *tile alphabet*, not "all and only" geometric
   tilings. Do not compare to A068599 or feed the exhaustiveness claim.
2. **Develop skipped.** These are the solver+pruner **combinatorial** k=1 solutions. The geometric
   `develop` stage (which certifies realizability) is regular-only, so composite counts are not
   develop-verified.

## The two families

Generated by `scripts/gen-composable-family.ts` (exact ℤ[ζ₁₂] boundary-enumeration + an exhaustive,
exact edge-to-edge dissectability oracle, `lib/classes/algorithm/composable/`). Preview PNGs:
`composable-family-preview-2026-07-11.png`, `composable-family-dissections-2026-07-11.png`.

- **Family B (convex):** all 11 convex unit-edge tiles with interior angles in {60,90,120,150}.
- **Family A (decomposable):** the 7 of those that genuinely tile into edge-to-edge unit regular
  {3,4,6,12} pieces. A ⊆ B. The 4 in B∖A are convex on the 30° grid but provably NOT unions of
  regular tiles.

| side count | convex (B) | decomposable (A) | in B∖A (convex-only) |
|---|---|---|---|
| 4 | rhombus | rhombus | — |
| 5 | house | house | — |
| 6 | 3 | hexagon C | hexagon A `[3,5,3,5,3,5]`, hexagon B `[3,4,5,3,4,5]` |
| 7 | heptagon | heptagon | — |
| 8 | 3 | octagon B | octagon A `[4,5,4,5,4,5,4,5]`, octagon C `[3,5,5,5,3,5,5,5]` |
| 9 | nonagon | nonagon | — |
| 10 | decagon | decagon | — |
| **total** | **11** | **7** | **4** |

## Alphabet size (gate, from gen_alphabet.py)

| palette | tiles | corner classes | vertex configs | vertexdef entries |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| regular {3,4,6,12} | 4 | 4 | 14 | 44 |
| composite-decomp (reg + A) | 11 | 33 | 8489 | 10017 |
| composite-convex (reg + B) | 15 | 44 | 18969 | 21626 |

No explosion (well under the 200k ceiling). Bounded because the smallest corner angle is 60° (2
units), capping vertex-word length at 6. A future 30°-corner tile would loosen this.

## k=1 counts

| palette | raw solver blocks | k=1 kept (WL-deduped) |
|---|---|---|
| regular {3,4,6,12} (reference) | — | 10 (octagon-blind; = 11 − t1002) |
| composite-decomp (reg + A) | 24 | **23** |
| composite-convex (reg + B) | 31 | **30** |

The 7 decomposable composites add 13 combinatorial 1-uniform solutions over regular-only (10 → 23).
The 4 non-decomposable convex tiles add a further **7** (23 → 30). That gap of 7 is the measured
difference between "genuinely composable" and "convex on the grid".

## Engine changes that made this possible

- `gen_alphabet.py`: `composite` tile kind (corner classes = fundamental-period positions of the
  angle-word) + a `CLASS_PREV` reverse-successor table; dropped the `p ≤ 2` assert.
- `eu_solver.cpp` `checkpart`: direction-locking (pick `CLASS_NEXT`/`CLASS_PREV` from the first face
  step) so mirror-placed period-p>2 tiles verify. Regular catalog stays byte-identical
  (`make check-regular` PASS: k1-6 = 10/20/61/151/332/673).
