\begin{abstract}
\noindent For edge-to-edge tilings of the plane by unit-edge regular polygons
($3,4,6,12$-gons, plus the analytically solved $4.8.8$ octagon tiling), we prove exact
values and proven search radii for $W(T)$ --- the least possible maximum step-weight of a
basis of the period lattice $\Lambda$ --- in the regime $k \le 3$ that the $pgg$ ceiling
document does not cover. Main results: (i) branch-wise upper bounds, by the shape of
$\Lambda$, each either a short lemma or a certified finite computation in exact arithmetic;
(ii) exactness: $\max W = 5, 6, 7$ at $k = 1, 2, 3$, attained by $p6m$ hexagonal lattices,
with length-based lower-bound certificates; (iii) a consumption analysis showing the
existing tuned $k=3$ enumeration pool is \emph{not} covered by these bounds (both its weight
radius and its Euclidean caps clip proven shells), so the certified $k=3$ run must be
re-executed at the proven configuration. The key device is that for rigid (hexagonal or
square) lattices the minimal vector's exact norm is pinned by the cell area, the admissible
areas form a finite nonnegative census, and each exact norm shell of $\ZZ[\zt{12}]$ is
finite --- so the density of the ring, fatal to length-based arguments, is irrelevant. No
enumeration data is used in any proof; the oracle appears only as a posteriori confirmation.
\end{abstract}

\section{Setting and statement}\label{sec:setting}

Let $T$ be a $k$-uniform edge-to-edge tiling of $\RR^2$ by unit-edge regular polygons of
the family $\{3,4,6,12\}$, $k \le 3$, with full symmetry group $G$, point group $P$, and
period lattice $\Lambda$. The octagon is excluded from the family: an octagon forces the
unique $4.8.8$ tiling, which is $1$-uniform (settled decision; its lattice is handled
analytically in \cref{thm:main}). Consequently all edge directions lie on one $30^\circ$
grid and $\Lambda \subset \ZZ[\zt{12}]$; weights are step-counts in the $12$-direction
alphabet ($24$ directions only for $4.8.8$). $W(\Lambda)$ is the minimum over bases of
$\Lambda$ of the maximum weight of a basis vector; a search pool of weight radius $s$
captures $\Lambda$ by \emph{pairs} iff $W(\Lambda) \le s$, and by \emph{generators + joins}
iff $\Lambda$ is generated by vectors of weight $\le s$.

\begin{theorem}[small-$k$ radii, per branch]\label{thm:main}
Every period lattice $\Lambda$ of a $k$-uniform tiling, $k \le 3$, satisfies the bound of
its row:
\begin{center}
\begin{tabular}{lcccl}
\toprule
branch (shape of $\Lambda$) & $k=1$ & $k=2$ & $k=3$ & form consumed by the solver\\
\midrule
hexagonal & $6$ & $8$ & $10$ & basis $(u, \omega u)$: pairs suffice\\
square & $3$ & $6$ & $7$ & basis $(u, iu)$: pairs suffice\\
$\hol(\Lambda) \le 4$ & $7$ & $15$ & $23$ & generators in $W(2n{-}1)$ + join closure\\
$4.8.8$ ($k=1$, $\zt{24}$) & $3$ & --- & --- & $u = 1{+}\sqrt2$, basis $(u, iu)$, analytic\\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\end{center}
\end{theorem}

\begin{theorem}[exactness]\label{thm:exact}
$\max_{T,\, k\text{-uniform}} W(\Lambda(T)) = 5, 6, 7$ at $k = 1, 2, 3$, attained by $p6m$
tilings with hexagonal lattices ($4.6.12$ at $k=1$); certificates in \cref{sec:attain}.
\end{theorem}

These replace, at $k \le 3$, the only previously proven radius $24k-1$
(\texttt{thm:weight}), whose $k=1$ pool was measured past $1.5\cdot10^8$ candidate values
(budget abort). Pool sizes here are tame: $|W(10)| = 18{,}481$ and $|W(23)| = 458{,}713$ in
$12$ directions, before norm filters.

\section{Inputs cited from the thesis}\label{sec:inputs}

Proven there, used as black boxes here: \texttt{lem:wallpaper} ($k$-uniform tilings have a
wallpaper group, in particular a rank-$2$ period lattice; and distinct vertices are at
distance $\ge 1$, so $\lambda_1 \ge 1$); \texttt{lem:quotient} ($|V(Q)| \le k\,|P|$, where
$Q$ is the graph of vertex classes mod $\Lambda$); \texttt{lem:voltage} and
\texttt{thm:weight} ($\Lambda$ is generated by fundamental-cycle voltages of $Q$, each of
weight $\le 2|V(Q)|-1$, each edge contributing one unit root); the bound $|P| \le
\hol(\Lambda)$ (inside \texttt{prop:orbitfloor}: $P$ is the group of linear parts, injective
since $\Lambda$ carries all translations of $G$, and preserves $\Lambda$ since $\Lambda
\trianglelefteq G$, so $P$ embeds in the holohedry as a subgroup --- whence, by Lagrange,
the stronger ``$|P|$ divides $\hol(\Lambda)$'' used below). The holohedry values
$\hol \in \{2,4,8,12\}$ (oblique; rectangular/rhombic; square; hexagonal) are the classical
Bravais classification; degenerate rhombic lattices at $60^\circ/90^\circ$ have holohedry
$12/8$ and belong to the hexagonal/square branches. Finally the corner-share identity:
$|{\det}\Lambda| = A = \sum_{v \in V(Q)} \mathrm{share}(\mathrm{VC}(v))$, where a corner of
a regular $m$-gon contributes $\mathrm{area}/m$. In coordinates $(p, q) \mathrel{\hat=}
(p+q\sqrt3)/12$ the shares are: triangle $(0,1)$, square $(3,0)$, hexagon $(0,3)$,
$12$-gon $(6,3)$ --- all nonnegative.

\section{The census lemmas}\label{sec:lemmas}

\begin{lemma}[orbit census]\label{lem:census}
Each of the $\le k$ vertex orbits under $G$ contributes $m_i = |P|/|\Stab_i|$ translation
classes, with $m_i$ dividing $|P|$, all of one VC species. Hence
$A = \sum_{i \le k} m_i\,\mathrm{share}(\mathrm{VC}_i)$ with $|P|$ dividing $\hol(\Lambda)$;
and on the quotient torus $V - E + F = 0$ with $E = \tfrac12\sum m_i \deg_i$ and
$F = \sum_p t_p$, $t_p = (\text{total $p$-corner count})/p \in \ZZ_{\ge 0}$. The species are
the $10$ multisets over $\{3,4,6,12\}$ with interior angles summing to $2\pi$.
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
$\Stab_G(v)$ is finite, meets $\Lambda$ trivially (nontrivial translations are
fixed-point-free), and injects into $P$; since $\Lambda \trianglelefteq G$, the
$\Lambda$-classes inside the orbit $Gv$ biject with $P/\overline{\Stab}(v)$, giving
$m_i = |P|/|\Stab_i|$, a divisor of $|P|$ by Lagrange. Distinct $G$-orbits have disjoint
class sets, so $n := |V(Q)| = \sum m_i$. The quotient is a genuine CW torus --- a nonzero
translation cannot fix a bounded tile (interior overlap) nor reverse an edge ($2v = 0$) ---
so the handshake and Euler identities apply verbatim, with loops and multi-edges handled by
the counting form. (Given angle-validity and $t_p \in \ZZ$, Euler holds identically; the
binding filters are the parity and per-$p$ integrality conditions --- Euler is kept as
belt-and-braces.) The species list is the classical around-a-point enumeration restricted
to $\{3,4,6,12\}$, re-derived mechanically in the artifact.
\end{proof}

\begin{lemma}[shell finiteness]\label{lem:shell}
For $x = a + b\zt{12} + c\zt{12}^2 + d\zt{12}^3 \in \ZZ[\zt{12}]$,
$|x|^2 = X + Y\sqrt3$ with $X = a^2{+}b^2{+}c^2{+}d^2{+}ac{+}bd \in \ZZ$ and
$Y = ab{+}bc{+}cd \in \ZZ$. The identity
$2X - (a^2{+}b^2{+}c^2{+}d^2) = (a{+}c)^2 + (b{+}d)^2 \ge 0$
gives $X \ge \tfrac12(a^2{+}b^2{+}c^2{+}d^2)$, so each exact shell
$\{x : |x|^2 = X + Y\sqrt3\}$ is finite, with coefficients bounded by $\sqrt{2X}$.
\end{lemma}

Density of $\ZZ[\zt{12}]$ in $\CC$ --- which defeats every Euclidean-radius argument --- is
irrelevant once the norm is pinned exactly. (Referee cross-check: the computed weights agree
with the independently derived closed form
$\wt = f(a_0,a_2) + f(a_1,a_3)$, $f(p,q) = \min_s |p{+}s| + |q{-}s| + |s|$, on all $4{,}140$
shell elements.)

\begin{lemma}[pure-species rigidity]\label{lem:rigid}
If every vertex of $T$ has species $s \in \{3^6,\ 4^4,\ 6^3,\ 3.12.12,\ 4.6.12\}$, then $T$
is the corresponding uniform tiling; in particular $k = 1$, with
$(n, \text{shell})$: $3^6\!: (1, (1,0)_{\mathrm{hex}})$; $4^4\!: (1, (1,0)_{\mathrm{sq}})$;
$6^3\!: (2, (3,0)_{\mathrm{hex}})$; $3.12.12\!: (6, (7,4)_{\mathrm{hex}})$;
$4.6.12\!: (12, (12,6)_{\mathrm{hex}})$.
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
$3^6/4^4/6^3$: single tile type; a regular tile placed on a given full edge, on a given
side, is unique, so the tiling is forced from any one tile. $3.12.12$: the vertex word
admits adjacent pairs $(3,12)$ and $(12,12)$ only, so no two triangles share an edge (every
neighbour of a triangle is a $12$-gon), and at each vertex of a $12$-gon its two edges
neighbour one $3$ and one $12$; hence edge-neighbours around every $12$-gon alternate
$3,12$ ($12$ edges, even --- consistent; the alternation phase of a $12$-gon is fixed by any
single one of its neighbours). $4.6.12$: pairs $(4,6), (6,12), (4,12)$ only, no same-type
pair; neighbours alternate $6,12$ around each square, $4,12$ around each hexagon, $4,6$
around each $12$-gon (all even edge counts; phase again fixed by one neighbour).
\emph{Propagation (both cases):} $T$ contains a $12$-gon (every vertex has one); the uniform
tiling $U$ is transitive on its $12$-gons, so position $U$ by an isometry matching one
$12$-gon of $T$. Induct on distance $r$ in the tile-adjacency graph from that tile: a tile
at distance $r{+}1$ shares a full edge with a matched tile $t$ at distance $r$; its
\emph{type} is forced (by $t$'s alternation pattern, whose phase was fixed when $t$ was
matched, or trivially when all of $t$'s neighbours have one type), and a regular tile of
known type on a known full edge and side is uniquely placed --- so it matches $U$. The
tile-adjacency graph is connected (\cref{lem:cooc}), so $T = U$; $U$ is vertex-transitive,
so $k = 1$. The tabulated $(n, \text{shell})$ values are computed from $U$'s known
structure (e.g.\ $3.12.12$: $n = 6$, $|u| = 2 + \sqrt3$, $|u|^2 = 7 + 4\sqrt3$).
\end{proof}

\begin{lemma}[co-occurrence connectivity]\label{lem:cooc}
Let $S$ be the set of species present in $T$ and $C(S)$ the graph on tile types with
$t \sim t'$ iff some $s \in S$ contains both. Then $C(S)$, restricted to the types present,
is connected. Census combinations with disconnected $C(S)$ are unrealizable.
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
Two tiles sharing an edge are both incident to both of its endpoints, so their types
co-occur in the endpoint VCs and are $C$-adjacent. For \emph{edge-to-edge, locally finite}
tilings by disks, the full-edge tile-adjacency graph is connected: perturb a path between
two tile interiors off the (discrete) vertex set; each transversal edge crossing switches
between the two tiles sharing that full edge. (The hypotheses matter --- the claim fails
for non-edge-to-edge tilings such as running-bond brick.) Both hold here. Connectivity of
the tile graph then forces connectivity of the type image.
\end{proof}

\begin{lemma}[rigid basis form and pinned shells]\label{lem:basis}
Let $\Lambda$ be hexagonal-shaped and $u$ a minimal vector. Then $\Lambda = \langle u,
\omega u\rangle$ with $\omega = e^{i\pi/3} = \zt{12}^2$, $\wt(\omega u) = \wt(u)$, and
$|u|^2 = \tfrac{2}{\sqrt3}A$. In census coordinates $A = (P_A + Q_A\sqrt3)/12$ this gives
$(X, Y) = (Q_A/6,\ P_A/18)$, nonnegative; combinations for which these fail to be integers
are unrealizable as hexagonal lattices (by \cref{lem:shell} a realizable $|u|^2$ has integer
$(X,Y)$) and are soundly dropped, remaining covered by the other branches' pools, which are
consumed as a union. Squares identically with $i = \zt{12}^3$, $|u|^2 = A$,
$(X, Y) = (P_A/12, Q_A/12)$; the $4.8.8$ case with $i = \zt{24}^6$.
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
$\hol(\Lambda)$ contains the $60^\circ$ rotation, so $\omega u \in \Lambda$ and
$\langle u, \omega u\rangle \subseteq \Lambda$. Equality: reduce any $x \in \Lambda$ modulo
$\langle u, \omega u\rangle$; the remainder lies within the covering radius $|u|/\sqrt3 <
|u| = \lambda_1$ of the triangular lattice $\langle u, \omega u\rangle$ and is itself in
$\Lambda$, hence is $0$. (Square case: covering radius $|u|/\sqrt2 < |u|$.) Multiplication
by a unit root permutes the step alphabet, so $\wt(\omega u) = \wt(u)$, and
$W(\Lambda) \le \wt(u)$. The norm pin is $A = |{\det}\Lambda| = \tfrac{\sqrt3}{2}|u|^2$
(hex) resp.\ $|u|^2$ (square); the coordinate arithmetic is immediate.
\end{proof}

\section{The computation (rigid branches)}\label{sec:comp}

Artifact: \texttt{experiments/results/smallk-census-shells-2026-07-10.py} (+ output CSV),
exact integer arithmetic throughout, $\sqrt3$ carried as coordinate pairs. Pipeline:
enumerate census combinations per \cref{lem:census} ($|P|$ over divisors of $12$ resp.\ $8$;
$r \le k$ orbits; $m_i \mid |P|$; parity, per-$p$ integrality, Euler), drop pure-rigid
multi-orbit combinations (\cref{lem:rigid}) and $C$-disconnected ones (\cref{lem:cooc}), add
the five true rigid shells; map to shells per \cref{lem:basis}; enumerate every shell
element (\cref{lem:shell} coefficient bound, boundary asserted); compute $\wt$ exactly by
breadth-first search over the $12$ unit directions (all shells resolved by depth $10$);
report the per-shell \emph{maximum} (sound: the tiling's $u$ is \emph{some} element of its
shell; all six minimal vectors share one weight, but distinct rotation classes on one shell
may differ --- the maximum covers all).

\begin{center}
\begin{tabular}{lccc}
\toprule
max shell weight & $k \le 1$ & $k \le 2$ & $k \le 3$\\
\midrule
hexagonal & $6$ & $8$ & $10$\\
square & $3$ & $6$ & $7$\\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\end{center}

Sanity pins: the $4.6.12$ shell $(12,6)$ has weight exactly $5$, its measured $W$. The
$k{=}1$ hexagonal maximum $6$ is attained only by shell $(14,0)$, whose sole census
provenance (re-derived by hand: at $r{=}1$ the only $m\cdot q = 84$ with $m \mid 12$ is
$m = 12$, $q = 7$) is a pure-$3^4.6$ orbit with $m = 12$; the real snub tiling is $p6$ with
$m = 6$ --- killing this needs a snub-uniqueness lemma, deliberately not claimed, hence $6$
rather than $5$. The square column carries the same kind of slack (its $k{=}1$ value $3$
comes from a phantom $m{=}8$ pure-$3^3.4^2$ combination; the real maxima are $1$ and $2$).

\section{The non-rigid branch ($\hol \le 4$)}\label{sec:nonrigid}

$|P| \le \hol(\Lambda) \le 4$ gives $n = |V(Q)| \le 4k$, so \texttt{thm:weight} yields
generators of weight $\le 2n - 1 = 7,\ 15,\ 23$ at $k = 1, 2, 3$, and \cref{lem:census}
bounds the cell area by $A \le 4k\,s_{\max}$ with $s_{\max} = (12+7\sqrt3)/12 \approx 2.01$
the maximal VC share ($A \lesssim 24.1$ at $k = 3$; contrast \texttt{cor:box}(ii)'s
$24k\,a_{\max} \approx 806$, where $a_{\max} = 3(2{+}\sqrt3)$ is the $12$-gon \emph{area} ---
distinct constants). The join-closure procedure of \texttt{cor:box}(iv) then runs over
$W(2n{-}1)$ filtered by $|{\det}| \le 24.1$ and the Gauss bounds. This branch is proven with
zero new mathematics but is the loose one (the measured non-rigid maximum at $k \le 3$ is
$6$); the named tightening is the quotient-first sweep over $|V(Q)| \le 12$ (work item
ST-6), which computes $W$ per voltage lattice directly and bypasses the pool entirely.

\section{Attainment: exactness of $5, 6, 7$}\label{sec:attain}

Lower bound: every basis vector of $\Lambda$ is a nonzero lattice vector, and each step has
unit length, so $\wt(x) \ge |x| \ge \lambda_1$; hence $W(\Lambda) \ge \lceil \lambda_1
\rceil$. For the argmax tilings (oracle ids; $T$-vectors are $\zt{12}$-coefficient tuples
from the catalogue; hexagonality $\omega T_1 \in \ZZ T_1 + \ZZ T_2$ verified exactly in each
case, so $\lambda_1 = |T_1|$):

\begin{center}
\begin{tabular}{clccccc}
\toprule
$k$ & tiling & $\lambda_1^2$ (exact) & $\lambda_1$ & $W \ge$ & shell max $\wt$ & $W =$\\
\midrule
$1$ & $4.6.12$ (\texttt{01\_46c-3c-1}) & $12+6\sqrt3$ & $4.732$ & $5$ & $5$ & $\mathbf{5}$\\
$2$ & \texttt{02\_346c-3c\_4l-1} ($p6m$) & $16+8\sqrt3$ & $5.464$ & $6$ & $6$ & $\mathbf{6}$\\
$3$ & \texttt{03\_346c-3a\_4a\_4l-1} ($p6m$) & $21+12\sqrt3$ & $6.464$ & $7$ & $7$ & $\mathbf{7}$\\
$3$ & \texttt{03\_346c-3c\_4h\_4k-1} ($p6m$) & $28+10\sqrt3$ & $6.732$ & $7$ & $7$ & $\mathbf{7}$\\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\end{center}

Each tiling's minimal-vector shell has computed maximum weight equal to its lower bound, so
the basis $(T_1, \omega T_1)$ realizes $W$ exactly; combined with \cref{thm:main} the maxima
over all $k \le 3$ tilings are exactly $5, 6, 7$, proving \cref{thm:exact}. (Consistent with
the oracle measurement over all $92$ tilings, which is used nowhere in the proofs.)

\section{Consumption by the solver}\label{sec:consumption}

The enumerator's \texttt{poolConfig} has a proven mode ($\text{poolSteps} = 24k{-}1$,
computationally dead) and a tuned mode ($\text{poolSteps} = 8$ at $k=3$), the latter below
the proven hexagonal need of $10$: \emph{the existing $k=3$ recertification (61/61) is not
covered by this theorem}. Moreover the weight radius alone is not sufficient: the tuned
pool also clips by Euclidean caps ($\text{poolLmax} \approx 8.12$ at $k = 3$; the $|w|^2$
caps at $66$), while the largest proven $k{=}3$ hexagonal shell is $(40,22)$ with
$|u| \approx 8.84$, $|u|^2 \approx 78.1$ --- a run with only poolSteps raised still loses
shells $(40,22), (44,16), (44,14), (40,16)$. The certified configuration must either set
$\text{poolLmax} \ge 8.84$ (hex; $\ge 5.99$ square) and lift the $|w|^2$ caps accordingly,
or bypass the pool with the explicit shell lists; the non-rigid branch additionally needs
the generator-and-join pool $W(23) \cap \{|v| \le \tfrac{2}{\sqrt3}\cdot 24.1\}$.

\section{Adversarial verification}\label{sec:adversarial}

Three referee subagents attacked the source document independently (orbit/lattice theory;
combinatorial geometry; computation audit), instructed to refute. \emph{No fatal findings.}
Survivals include: the census's faithfulness to \cref{lem:census} through the non-symmorphic
groups; the $10$-species enumeration; the BFS weights, cross-checked against an
independently derived closed form on all $4{,}140$ shell elements and against a second
referee's reimplementation (CSV byte-identical); all five rigid shells and both attainment
pins re-derived; the branch split's exhaustiveness (union-of-pools consumption makes shape
misclassification harmless); pool sizes exact. Gaps found and repaired in v2: the
lattice-equality step of \cref{lem:basis} (now the covering-radius argument); the
propagation induction of \cref{lem:rigid} (was a clause, now written); the hypotheses of
\cref{lem:cooc} (the brick counterexample); the Euclidean-cap requirement of
\cref{sec:consumption} --- the one finding that could have reproduced a real completeness
hole downstream; citation and symbol fixes ($s_{\max}$ vs $a_{\max}$).
